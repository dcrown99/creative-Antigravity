# Session Breakout Strategy - Asian Range to London Open

## Academic Foundation
- Gao, Han, Li, Zhou (2018) "Market Intraday Momentum", Journal of Financial Economics 129(2), 394-414
- Significant positive relationship between early and late half-hour returns within a trading day
- Effect is more pronounced during financial crises and when market concentration due to trading hours is a factor
- Intraday momentum is influenced by risk aversion among liquidity providers regarding overnight holdings

## Alpha Source Analysis
- The London session accounts for 38% of global FX turnover (UK alone)
- Asian session: low liquidity, narrow range consolidation (00:00-07:59 UTC)
- London open: massive institutional order flow influx creating directional momentum
- The transition from low-volatility Asian to high-volatility London creates predictable breakouts
- Institutional delta hedging and portfolio rebalancing at session opens

## Strategy Rules (from MQL5 community and practitioners)
- Asian Range: Track high/low from 00:00 to 07:59 UTC (server time)
- Entry Window: London Open 08:00-08:05 UTC
- Entry Signal: 5-minute candle closes outside Asian session high/low
- Volume Filter: Breakout candle volume > 2x the 20-period average volume
- Risk: 0.5% per trade
- Stop Loss: 20 pips beyond breakout level
- Take Profit: 60 pips (3:1 RR)

## Backtest Evidence (from practitioners)
- GBP/JPY: 80% annual return, 15% max DD, 31.5% WR, 4:1 RR
- London breakout: 7.47% return in 10 months, Sharpe 1.84, 49% WR
- Asian gold breakout: PF 7.294 over 30 days (70.59% WR)
- London open: 71% WR with 1:1 RR reported

## Why This Edge Persists (Structural)
1. UK/London institutional flow is structural - won't disappear
2. Asian session is fundamentally low-liquidity due to timezone (structural)
3. The volatility transition is driven by real institutional order flow, not patterns
4. Retail traders can exploit this because HFT doesn't dominate session-level timeframes
